A Ghost is a Cruel Name for an Angel
by Jake Hoffman

“Same quilt in life, same grave in death”
What we have isn’t to be held,
But lowered.

The underworld could be something new for us
To watch,
     I watch

As you ascend instead.

I wasn’t crying,
I was staring and forgot to blink.

The golden sky, the morning birds,
it all took you
from me. And our pit of hell.

Why do I have nothing again,
don't I always have you?
Where have you
gone.

Did they erect temples for you?
I surely couldn’t
bring myself to sculpt that calm satisfaction, look in your eyes
of porcelain

A look knowing
so confidently knowing that I was yours
I belonged to you
belong to you still,
Tying a knot around every good deed

The door you hold open,
         (thanks)
and maybe I could join you,
up above.

But I chose something new for us

I was loving,
giving my essence,
dissecting myself before you,
burying myself within you.
We have the same grave in death, now
But that statue wasn’t of me,

  was it?


But then:   
From my tomb of us,   
From my personal ‘change of scenery’   
Which you were too good for, I noticed   

you would descend from gods,   
Not to my depths   
But the surface   

Where we built everything we had   
And how all of it is nothing without you.   

You would swoop down, to the temple of you   
There was no calming look in your eyes this time,   
only a vision   

Trying to remember the shape of my back   
Forming the beauty mark behind my left ear   
Caressing, that is chiseled from stone.    

they erect temples for you?
I surely couldn’t


But I watched you.   
only when I was back in your view, sharing a monument,   
beside you eternally   
did the look in your eyes resurrect.   

The look I could never get mad at   
The one that makes you the angel    

and I
a ghost.

“Same quilt in life, same grave in death”

And same shrine in rest
